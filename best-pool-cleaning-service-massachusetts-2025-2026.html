What customers actually care about

After five years of servicing pools from Saugus to Hamilton, from Lynn to Topsfield, we've found that what customers value most isn't fancy equipment or the cheapest price. It's much simpler than that:

Reliability. They want someone who shows up when they say they will. Not "sometime this week" — Thursday morning means Thursday morning.
Communication. Return the call. Answer the text. Don't leave people waiting and wondering.
Honesty. If the pump is fine, say the pump is fine. Don't create problems to sell solutions.
Competence. Know the difference between a chemistry problem and an equipment problem. Our CPO certification isn't just a badge — it means we understand the science behind pool care.
Respect. Treat their property like you'd treat your own. Clean up. Don't leave a mess. Don't trample the flower beds.

None of this is revolutionary. But in an industry where half the competition doesn't return phone calls, doing the basics well is apparently enough to earn back-to-back "Best of" awards across all of Southern New England.

What to look for when choosing a pool service in Massachusetts

Whether you're in Beverly, Salem, Marblehead, Wakefield, or anywhere on the North Shore, here's what you should look for before hiring a pool company:

Check their Google Reviews. Not just the star rating — read what people actually say. Look for mentions of communication, reliability, and honesty. A company with 4.8 stars and detailed reviews is more trustworthy than one with a perfect 5.0 and three reviews.

Ask about CPO certification. A Certified Pool Operator has passed a nationally recognized exam covering water chemistry, equipment operation, and safety. Not every technician needs to be certified, but the company should have at least one CPO on staff who oversees service quality.

Get a straight answer on pricing. A reputable company will give you a clear quote before starting work. If they're vague about pricing or won't commit to a number, that's a red flag.

Test their responsiveness. Call them. If they don't answer, do they call back within 48 hours? The way a company handles your first call tells you everything about how they'll handle your pool.
